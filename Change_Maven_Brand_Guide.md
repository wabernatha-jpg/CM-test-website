# Change Maven LLC — Brand Identity Guide
**Version 1.0 · 2026**
*Confidential — Internal Use Only*

---

## 01 · Introduction

This guide defines the visual and verbal identity of Change Maven LLC. It exists to ensure that every touchpoint — from proposals to the website to business cards — communicates a consistent, professional, and approachable brand.

Change Maven is built on the belief that great change management is fundamentally about people and relationships. Our brand should reflect that: warm but credible, collaborative but confident, locally rooted but nationally capable.

These guidelines are a living document and should be updated as the brand evolves.

---

## 02 · Brand Philosophy

### Mission
To guide organizations through change — aligning people, process, and technology so transformation sticks.

### Vision
To be the most trusted change management partner for mid-market companies, government agencies, and nonprofits across the Midwest and beyond.

### Brand Personality
Change Maven's personality is defined by five core traits:

- **Approachable** — We meet clients where they are, speak plainly, and build genuine relationships.
- **Collaborative** — We coach and guide; we never impose. The client's team owns the outcome.
- **Credible** — Our credentials, experience, and track record earn trust before we ask for it.
- **Grounded** — We are rooted in Alton, Illinois, and proud of our Midwestern work ethic.
- **Transformative** — We help organizations become meaningfully better, not just different.

### Brand Promise
> *From where you are · to where you need to be.*

This tagline captures the essence of what Change Maven does: we take organizations on a managed, supported journey from their current state to a better future state. It is approachable, confident, and outcome-focused.

## 03 · Logo

### Primary Logo
The Change Maven logo combines the Clark Bridge silhouette — an iconic landmark from Alton, Illinois — with the company wordmark. The bridge serves as a metaphor for transformation: connecting an organization's current state to where it needs to be.

The bridge appears as an angled cable-stay structure in the background, with the **CHANGE MAVEN** wordmark prominently in front. The two towers anchor the composition and give the logo a sense of strength and forward movement.

### Logo Variations

| Variation | Usage |
|-----------|-------|
| **Primary (dark)** | Navy background, gold bridge, cream wordmark — proposals, printed materials, dark surfaces |
| **Reversed (light)** | White/cream background, navy wordmark, gold bridge — letterheads, light digital backgrounds |
| **Icon only** | Bridge silhouette alone — social media profiles, favicons, embossed materials |
| **Wordmark only** | CHANGE MAVEN LLC in navy, no bridge — space-limited contexts |

### Clear Space
Always maintain clear space around the logo equal to the height of the letter **"C"** in CHANGE. No other text, images, or graphic elements should appear within this zone.

### Minimum Size
- **Digital:** 120px wide minimum (full logo)
- **Print:** 1.5 inches wide minimum (full logo)
- The icon-only version may be used at smaller sizes.

### Logo Don'ts
- Do not stretch, skew, or distort the logo
- Do not recolor outside of approved color variations
- Do not place on busy photographic backgrounds without an opacity mask
- Do not add drop shadows, outlines, or effects
- Do not recreate in a different typeface

## 04 · Color Palette

The Change Maven palette is rooted in authority, warmth, and trust. Navy anchors the brand in professionalism. Gold conveys confidence and forward momentum. Cream softens the palette and adds approachability.

### Primary Colors

| Color | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|-------|-----|-----|-------|
| **Navy** | `#0D1B2A` | 13, 27, 42 | Primary background, dominant brand color |
| **Gold** | `#C9982A` | 201, 152, 42 | Accent — headings, rules, highlights, CTAs |
| **Cream** | `#F7F4EF` | 247, 244, 239 | Secondary background, print materials |
| **Slate** | `#4A5568` | 74, 85, 104 | Body copy on light backgrounds |
| **Gold Light** | `#E8B84B` | 232, 184, 75 | Hover states, secondary accents |

### Color Usage Rules
- **Navy** is the dominant background color for hero sections, proposals, and primary brand materials
- **Gold** is the accent color — use for headings, rules, callouts, and highlights. Never use as body text on white
- **Cream** is the primary background for printed materials, proposals, and secondary digital sections
- **Slate** is used for body copy on light backgrounds
- **Gold Light** is used for hover states and decorative elements only

### Accessibility
- Navy + Cream: WCAG AAA compliant
- Navy + Gold: WCAG AA compliant
- Always test contrast ratios before introducing new color combinations

## 05 · Typography

Change Maven uses two typefaces: one for editorial and display use, and one for body copy and UI.

### Display Typeface — Libre Baskerville
**Use for:** headlines, hero text, section titles, pull quotes, and the logo wordmark.

Libre Baskerville is a serif typeface that communicates authority, credibility, and warmth. Its slightly humanist character reflects our collaborative, people-first approach.

| Style | Weight | Use Case |
|-------|--------|----------|
| Libre Baskerville Bold | 700 | Headings, section titles |
| Libre Baskerville Regular | 400 | Subheadings, body display |
| Libre Baskerville Italic | 400i | Pull quotes, emphasis |

### Body Typeface — Inter
**Use for:** body copy, captions, labels, navigation, UI elements, and all digital text below heading level.

Inter is a clean, highly legible sans-serif designed for screens. It pairs well with Baskerville and keeps body copy easy to read at any size.

| Style | Weight | Use Case |
|-------|--------|----------|
| Inter Regular | 400 | Body copy — 16px / 11pt minimum |
| Inter Medium | 500 | Labels, captions — uppercase, tracked |
| Inter SemiBold | 600 | UI emphasis, strong labels |

### Fallback Stacks
- **Display fallback:** Georgia, Times New Roman, serif
- **Body fallback:** system-ui, -apple-system, Arial, sans-serif

### Typography Don'ts
- Do not use more than two typefaces in a single document
- Do not use decorative or script fonts in any Change Maven materials
- Do not set body copy below 10pt in print or 14px on screen
- Do not use all-caps for body text — only for labels and eyebrow text

## 06 · Voice & Tone

### Brand Voice
Change Maven's voice is consistent across all communications — proposals, website, emails, and presentations.

**Our voice is:**
- **Conversational but credible** — we speak like a trusted advisor, not a textbook
- **Direct and clear** — we say what we mean without jargon or filler
- **Collaborative** — we use "we" and "together" language wherever appropriate
- **Confident without arrogance** — we show substance, not self-promotion

### Tone by Context

| Context | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Proposals & formal documents | Professional, precise, outcome-focused. Lead with the client's challenge. |
| Website | Warm, direct, approachable. Speak to the COO worried about a rollout failing. |
| Social media | Conversational, insight-driven, never salesy. Share knowledge generously. |
| Emails | Clear and concise. Respect the reader's time. |

### Voice Examples

| Context | ✓ Say this | ✗ Not this |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| Website headline | Every change. Fully managed. | Comprehensive organizational transformation solutions. |
| Proposal intro | Here's what we heard from you, and how we'd approach it. | Pursuant to your Request for Proposal dated... |
| Email sign-off | Let me know what questions you have. | Please do not hesitate to contact me should you require further information. |
| Describing services | We coach your team through how to prioritize. | We leverage best-in-class methodologies to optimize change adoption. |

### Words We Use
- Change, transform, guide, coach, align, together, straightforward, practical

### Words We Avoid
- Synergy, leverage, scalable solutions, robust, cutting-edge, best-in-class, paradigm shift

## 07 · Stationery & Templates

### Business Card
- **Front:** Logo (primary dark version), name, title
- **Back:** Navy background, tagline in gold, website URL, contact email
- **Specs:** Standard 3.5" × 2", matte finish preferred

### Letterhead
- Logo top-left on cream background
- Company name, address, and contact info in Slate at 9pt, page bottom
- Gold rule separating header from body content

### Proposal Template
- **Cover page:** Full navy background, logo centered, client name, project title, date in gold
- **Section dividers:** Gold rule with section number and title in Libre Baskerville Bold
- **Body:** Cream background, Inter body copy, callout boxes in light navy with gold border
- **Footer:** Page number centered, website URL right-aligned

### Email Signature
```
[Name]                         ← Libre Baskerville Bold, 14px, Navy
[Title]                        ← Inter Medium, 12px, Slate
Change Maven LLC               ← Inter Regular, 12px, Gold
[Phone] · [Email] · [Website]  ← Inter Regular, 11px, Slate
[Logo icon — 48px]
```

## 08 · Digital Guidelines

### Website
- **Primary background:** Navy `#0D1B2A` for hero and key sections
- **Secondary background:** Cream `#F7F4EF` for content sections
- **CTA buttons:** Gold fill, Navy text, 2px border-radius
- **Hover states:** Gold Light `#E8B84B`
- **Body text:** Slate on light backgrounds; 90% white opacity on dark

### Social Media
- **Profile image:** Bridge icon on Navy background
- **Cover images:** Navy background with Gold rule and brand tagline
- **Post graphics:** Maintain color palette and typeface — no clip art or stock overlays
- **Primary platform:** LinkedIn — professional, insight-driven content

### Presentations
- **Title slides:** Navy background, white/cream text, Gold accents
- **Content slides:** Cream or white background, Navy headings, Slate body
- **Charts & diagrams:** Navy for primary data, Gold for highlights
- Never use default PowerPoint or Google Slides color themes

## 09 · Brand Don'ts

These are absolute rules — they protect the integrity and consistency of the Change Maven brand.

- Do not use clip art, stock illustrations, or generic icons inconsistent with the brand aesthetic
- Do not use the logo on backgrounds that reduce legibility
- Do not mix typefaces outside of the approved pair
- Do not use brand colors in proportions that reduce Navy as the dominant color
- Do not use the bridge imagery in a way that appears casual or cartoonish
- Do not create marketing materials without reviewing against this guide
- Do not use competitor logos or brand materials in Change Maven presentations without explicit permission
